  <how-to-verify>
Review the generated Texas State questions for quality, accuracy, and mixed-durability compliance. Focus on the known pitfall areas from the research:

1. **Mixed-durability check (NEW for this collection):**
   - Confirm the set contains BOTH durable and expiring questions
   - Expiring questions (current officeholders like Abbott, Patrick, Paxton, Jane Nelson) should have expiresAt: "2027-01-19T00:00:00Z"
   - Durable questions (Capitol height, Republic of Texas, Railroad Commission name, two courts of last resort) should have expiresAt: null
   - No fixed ratio required -- just verify both types exist

2. **Factual accuracy checks (blocking):**
   - No question says "annual sessions" -- Texas has BIENNIAL regular sessions (odd years, 140-day limit)
   - No question attributes railroad regulation to the Railroad Commission -- it regulates oil/gas since 2005
   - No question describes the Texas Supreme Court as the highest court for criminal cases -- that is the Court of Criminal Appeals
   - No question references a specific Texas city (Dallas, Houston, San Antonio, Fort Worth, El Paso, Plano, Arlington) as the subject of a question -- state-only rule
   - Austin STATE institutions (Capitol, Governor's Mansion) are acceptable; Austin CITY facts are not

3. **Comptroller accuracy (special attention):**
   - Glenn Hegar resigned February 2025; Kelly Hancock is acting Comptroller
   - If any question asks "Who is the current Comptroller?", verify accuracy or archive it
   - Questions about the Comptroller's ROLE and POWERS (certifying the budget, fiscal estimates) are safe and preferred

4. **Historical accuracy:**
   - Republic of Texas: 1836-1845 (9 years)
   - Sam Houston: President of the Republic twice, first Governor, removed 1861 for refusing Confederate oath
   - Texas Capitol: 302.64 ft (vs. US Capitol 288 ft) -- 14.64 ft taller, NOT "15 ft" or other rounded numbers
   - Juneteenth: June 19, 1865, Galveston -- federal holiday since 2021

5. **Scope check:**
   - No questions about US Senators Ted Cruz or John Cornyn (federal subjects)
   - No questions about Texas sports teams, private companies, or universities as institutions
   - No question has an address, phone number, or budget figure as its answer
   - Civic policy questions (ERCOT, no income tax, water rights) frame the policy structurally -- what it IS, not partisan

6. **Quality/difficulty check:**
   - Questions feel like "dinner party" civic tidbits -- surprising and shareable
   - The Railroad Commission name/function mismatch, Capitol height, two courts of last resort, and Lt. Governor's power should be among the strongest questions
