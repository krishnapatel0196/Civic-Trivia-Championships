  <how-to-verify>
Review the generated Massachusetts State questions for quality and accuracy. Focus on the known pitfall areas from the research:

1. **Factual accuracy checks (blocking):**
   - No question calls the legislature "the state legislature," "state assembly," or "state congress" — it must be "the General Court"
   - No question describes the 1780 Constitution as "one of the oldest" — it must be "the world's oldest functioning written constitution still in use"
   - No question implies Massachusetts counties have elected executives or county commissions — most county governments were abolished in the late 1990s
   - No question asserts MCAS is currently required for high school graduation — this changed in late 2025
   - No question mentions Boston City Council or Boston Mayor as state government subjects — those are city, not state

2. **Governor's Council coverage:**
   - At least a few questions cover the Governor's Council (governors-council topic category) — this is one of MA's most distinctive civic structures and should be well-represented
   - Questions about the Governor's Council should describe it as an "elected" body, not "appointed"

3. **Expiring questions check:**
   - Questions naming specific current officials (Governor Healey, AG Campbell, Treasurer Goldberg, Secretary Galvin) should have expiresAt set to "2027-01-15T00:00:00Z"
   - Questions naming General Court members or Governor's Councillors should have expiresAt "2027-01-01T00:00:00Z"
   - Structural and historical questions should have expiresAt null

4. **Scope check:**
   - No questions about US Senators Warren or Markey as state subjects (they are federal)
   - No questions about private companies (Raytheon, Fidelity, etc.)
   - No questions about Boston sports teams or Harvard/MIT as institutions
   - Civic policy milestone questions (Chapter 58 health reform, Goodridge ruling) frame the milestone structurally and non-partisanly — what the policy/ruling DID, not party framing

5. **Quality/difficulty check:**
   - Questions feel like "dinner party" civic tidbits — surprising and shareable
   - No question has an address, phone number, or budget figure as its answer

**To review questions:** Use the admin UI or query the DB for questions with externalIdPrefix like 'mas%' and status = 'draft'.

**If you find issues:**
- Archive individual bad questions via the admin UI or the archive script
- If a specific factual topic has systemic errors (e.g., all General Court questions use the wrong name), archive that cluster and describe what needs fixing so Claude can adjust the stateFeatures and regenerate
