Source a high-quality photograph of the Massachusetts State House for the collection banner image.

**About the building:** The Massachusetts State House (24 Beacon Street, Boston) is a Federal-style building designed by Charles Bulfinch, completed in 1798. Its defining feature is the gold dome — originally covered in copper by Paul Revere's company in 1802, re-gilded with 23-karat gold in 1997. It is a National Historic Landmark. The gold dome is the most recognizable civic symbol of Massachusetts and the right subject for this banner.

**Source options (in priority order):**

1. **Wikimedia Commons (Creative Commons — preferred for quality):**
   - Search: https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Massachusetts_State_House
   - Filter for freely licensed images (CC-BY or CC-BY-SA or public domain)
   - Many high-quality exterior photos showing the full building with gold dome are available
   - Note the license for the SUMMARY (attribution may be required for CC-BY/CC-BY-SA)

2. **Library of Congress HABS (public domain — no attribution required):**
   - URL: https://www.loc.gov/pictures/search/?q=massachusetts+state+house
   - These are public domain

**Image requirements:**
- **Subject:** Exterior of the Massachusetts State House showing the gold dome — the civic landmark, not an interior shot
- **Aspect ratio:** Landscape, approximately 2:1 (check other banners: indiana-state.jpg, california-state.jpg are reference points)
- **Format:** JPG
- **File name:** `massachusetts-state.jpg`
- **Destination:** `frontend/public/images/collections/massachusetts-state.jpg`
- Crop or resize to match the landscape aspect ratio used by other banners if needed

**Steps:**
