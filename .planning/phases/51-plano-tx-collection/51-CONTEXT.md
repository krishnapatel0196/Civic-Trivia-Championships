<decisions>
## Implementation Decisions

### Topic backbone — Growth story anchors the collection
- Plano's arc from cotton-farming town to one of America's fastest-growing cities is the through-line
- Development decisions, city planning choices, and how Plano governed its growth are central topics
- Early history is strong durable material: Balloon Capital of Texas, Interurban railroad era, cotton farming roots, how Plano got its name — surprising and shareable tidbits
- Corporate presence (Toyota HQ, JCPenney, Legacy West) is included only at the civic angle: economic development policy, what major relocations meant for the city, tax agreements — not brand promotion

### Topic mix
- Growth story + city government structure together form the backbone
- City governance: council-manager structure, how Plano plans and zones, Collin County relationship, city services
- History: early Plano era, Balloon Capital designation, founding timeline, notable civic firsts
- Economic development: how and why companies chose Plano, Legacy West as civic development story — civic angle only
- Community identity: demographic change, Plano ISD excellence, diversity of the city — woven in as civic facts, not isolated as a separate topic

### Community representation
- Plano's large and growing Asian American and South Asian community is part of the civic story — woven in naturally through demographic facts, community institutions, cultural civic organizations
- Not a standalone topic with dedicated questions, but not omitted — referenced where the civic facts lead
- Same "feel seen" standard as Cambridge: the collection should represent the whole city, not just the affluent enclaves or corporate identity

### Corporate identity framing
- Companies are civic facts: what their arrival or departure meant for city development, employment, tax base, and city planning decisions
- No brand promotion or corporate cheerleading
- Legacy West is a civic development story, not a shopping destination

### Expiring vs. durable mix
- Target ~50/50: roughly half durable (structural, historical facts), half expiring (current mayor, city manager, recent council actions)
- Expiring: "Who is the current City Manager?" — designed to be refreshed
- Durable: growth history, civic firsts, government structure, geographic/demographic facts
- Pair expiring questions with durable counterparts in the same topic area

### Difficulty calibration
- Same calibration as Cambridge: dinner party test applies
- "Easy" = a civic-minded person would be proud to share this tidbit. If it feels like admin trivia (address, phone number, internal process), cut it
- Over-index on Easy questions — the Easy Steps design requires accessible entry points
- Never use questions whose answer is an address or phone number

### Anti-partisan framing
- Anti-partisan (not just non-partisan) — strip partisanship from questions entirely
- Focus on civic structures, what leaders did in their civic roles, historical facts — not political positioning or party affiliation
- Avoid framing questions around controversial ballot outcomes or endorsements

### Collection visual identity
- Banner image: Hot air balloon — ties directly to Plano's "Balloon Capital of Texas" historical identity
- Colorful and distinctive; contrasts well with the skyline/civic-building approach of other collections
- Slug: `plano-tx`

### Claude's Discretion
- Exact topic distribution percentages within the growth + city government framework
- Which specific Collin County relationship facts rise to the civic significance threshold
- How to handle questions about city demographics (balance specificity with non-address-trivia standard)
- Color theme for the collection card

</decisions>

## Specific Ideas

- Balloon Capital of Texas angle is the hook for early history — the annual balloon festival and the designation itself are model durable questions
- Corporate HQ relocations (Toyota moving North American HQ to Plano in 2017) are civic facts about economic development, not advertising
- Legacy West development is a city planning story, not a luxury destination story
- Plano ISD's reputation and its civic role in the city's growth story is fair game as a civic institution fact
- The council-manager form of government is Plano's structure — same civic emphasis as Cambridge's City Manager and Plan E charter
