Cambridge Living Wage Ordinance

Cambridge adopted its Living Wage Ordinance in May 1999. The ordinance requires employers who receive city contracts or financial assistance to pay their employees a living wage.

Key facts:
- Cambridge passed its living wage ordinance in May 1999 (NOT 1998)
- Baltimore was the first US city to pass a living wage law in 1994 — Cambridge was NOT the first
- The ordinance applies to city contractors and recipients of city financial assistance
- The living wage rate is updated periodically; at passage it was approximately $10.68/hour
- The City of Cambridge Purchasing Department administers the ordinance
- Covered workers include those employed by businesses receiving city contracts above a certain dollar threshold
- The ordinance was a significant progressive policy milestone for the city

The Cambridge living wage ordinance came about through advocacy by labor groups and community organizations. It was one of many similar ordinances passed by cities across the US in the late 1990s as part of a national living wage movement.
